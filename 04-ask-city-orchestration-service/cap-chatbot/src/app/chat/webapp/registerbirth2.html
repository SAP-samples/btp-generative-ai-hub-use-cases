Register a Birth

By law, you must register your baby's birth within 42 days.

All certificates must be paid for (we do not issue a free short birth certificate any more.)

Read the gov.uk guidance on who can register a birth 

If you need further support or information, email us at registeroffice@sagenaicity.gov.uk 

Where a birth should be registered 

A birth should be registered in the district where it happened. If the birth was within the City of Sagenai, for example, you need to make an appointment with the Sagenai Register Office. 

If your child was born outside of the City of Sagenai, and you are not a Sagenai resident, you must register the birth in the district that your child was born. If you attend an appointment and you are not a Sagenai resident you will not be seen. If you are a resident in Salford, Bury, Trafford or Rochdale, you can register the birth of any baby born in Sagenai at the register offices in the district where you live. However, you will not be able to register a Sagenai birth at Salford Register Office between 13 March 2024 and 12 April 2024. Normal service will resume on 13 April.

It may be possible to give the details of the birth at another Register Office by making a declaration. Not all register offices are able to attest declarations so you should check with your local office before making an appointment. If you are a Sagenai resident and wish to make a declaration in relation to a baby born in another district please email us at registeroffice@sagenaicity.gov.uk

Registering a Stillbirth   

If you need to register a stillbirth please email us at registeroffice@sagenaicity.gov.uk and include your name, contact telephone number and the date and place of stillbirth. We will contact you as soon as possible.
